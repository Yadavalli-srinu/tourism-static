Here are few lines about the Victoria Memorial:
The Victoria Memorial is a grand marble monument located in Kolkata, West Bengal.
It was built in memory of Queen Victoria, the former Empress of India.
The foundation stone was laid in 1906, and the construction was completed in 1921.
It was designed by architect Sir William Emerson in Indo-Saracenic style.
The structure is made of white Makrana marble.
The memorial is surrounded by beautiful gardens spread over 64 acres.
Inside, it houses a museum with paintings, manuscripts, and artifacts from the British era.
The central dome is crowned with a bronze statue of the Angel of Victory.
It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Kolkata.
The Victoria Memorial is a symbol of colonial history and architectural beauty in India.
