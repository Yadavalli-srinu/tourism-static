Golden Temple

Here are few lines about the Golden Temple:
The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar, Punjab.
It is the holiest shrine in Sikhism.
The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das in 1581 and completed by Guru Arjan Dev in 1604.
Its upper floors are covered with gold, giving it the name “Golden Temple.”
The temple is surrounded by a sacred water tank called the Amrit Sarovar.
The Guru Granth Sahib, the holy book of Sikhs, is kept inside the temple.
It is open to people of all religions and backgrounds.
The temple complex also houses the world’s largest free kitchen (langar), serving thousands daily.
The architecture blends Islamic, Hindu, and Rajput styles.
It is a major pilgrimage site and a symbol of peace, equality, and spiritual devotion.
