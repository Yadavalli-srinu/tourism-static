Hawa Mahal

Here are few lines about the Hawa Mahal:
The Hawa Mahal is a famous palace located in Jaipur, Rajasthan.
It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.
The architect of the palace was Lal Chand Ustad.
Its name means “Palace of Winds” or “Palace of Breeze.”
The structure is made of red and pink sandstone.
It has 953 small windows called jharokhas, decorated with intricate latticework.
The design allowed royal women to watch street processions without being seen.
It is shaped like the crown of Lord Krishna.
The Hawa Mahal is a five-story building with no front entrance from the outside; entry is from the back.
It is one of Jaipur’s most popular tourist attractions and an iconic landmark of Rajasthan.
