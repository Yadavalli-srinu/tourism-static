Charminar

Here are few lines about the Charminar:
The Charminar is a historic monument located in Hyderabad, India.
It was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah.
The name “Charminar” means “Four Minarets” in Hindi and Urdu.
It was constructed to commemorate the end of a deadly plague in the city.
The structure is made of granite, limestone, and mortar.
Each minaret is about 56 meters (184 feet) high.
The monument has four grand arches facing four different directions.
It is a blend of Indo-Islamic architecture with Persian influences.
The Charminar is surrounded by a bustling market called Laad Bazaar, famous for bangles.
It is one of the most popular tourist attractions and a symbol of Hyderabad.
