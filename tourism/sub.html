Gate way of india

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument completed in 1924 on the waterfront of Mumbai, India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of George V for his coronation as the Emperor of India in December 1911

HawaMahal

The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers

Kerala

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals. Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife

Golden temple

Amritsar, Punjab, is a city rich in culture and history, with the Golden Temple and the Wagah Border ceremony being its most prominent attractions. The Golden Temple is a sacred Sikh shrine, while the Wagah Border hosts a daily flag-lowering ceremony that draws large crowds.

Victoria memorial

The Queen Victoria Memorial is a large, white marble building in Kolkata, India, built as a memorial to Queen Victoria, the Empress of India. It was commissioned by Lord Curzon, the Viceroy of British India, and constructed between 1906 and 1921

Tirupati

is a city in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh and serves as the administrative headquarters of Tirupati district. It is known for its significant religious and cultural heritage, being home to the renowned Tirumala Venkateswara Temple,
