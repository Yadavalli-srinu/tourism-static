Here are few lines about the Gate way of india:
The Gateway of India is a famous monument located in Mumbai, Maharashtra.
It was built during the British rule in 1924.
The structure overlooks the Arabian Sea at Apollo Bunder.
It was designed by architect George Wittet in Indo-Saracenic style.
The monument was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.
It is made of yellow basalt and reinforced concrete.
The central arch is 26 meters (85 feet) high.
The Gateway was the ceremonial entrance for Viceroys and Governors to India.
In 1948, the last British troops departed India through this gateway.
Today, it is a popular tourist spot and a symbol of Mumbai.
